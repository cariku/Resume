My name is Carolyn Hirsch. I am currently a full-time student at William Paterson University with a major in Graphic and Interactive Design. I will graduate this May 2020. I also work part-time as a barista at Starbucks.

A majority of my artwork consists of commercial type designs such as packaging and advertising. There is a large focus on color and typographic elements within my work. My prefered style is minimalistic that is both clean and contemporary.

With commercial-type designs such as packaing and advertising, the main goal of any project is to draw the eye. One of the elements I use to achieve that is the use of bright, lively colors. Following the basic principles of design known as the Laws of Gestalt, I can create a piece that is visually appealing and successful. Another element that I focus on is typography. Graphic design can be about experimentation; it does not always have to follow strict rules. I use type as both a form of providing information but also to create a more cohesive feeling to a design by using it as a design element both literally and unconventionally.

My artwork is meant to encapsulate the main ideas of Graphic Design with a larger focus on color and typography. Within my art, I wish to create a reaction in the viewer and to spread a message.
